# What is operator overloading in C++, and why should you care?

C++ allows you to *customize the behavior* or the following *operators* in the *same way you can overload functions*:

`+`, `-`, `*`, `/`, `%`, `^`, `&`, `|`, `~`, `!`, `=`, `<`, `>`, `+=`, `-=`, `*=`, `/=`, `%=`, `^=`, `&=`, `|=`, `<<`, `>>`, `>>=`, `<<=`, `==`, `!=`, `<=`, `>=`, `<=>` (since C++20), `&&`, `||`, `++`, `--`, `,`, `->*`, `->`, `( )`, and `[ ]`
